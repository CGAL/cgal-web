The CGAL developers teams wishes you a happy year!

2025 was a fruitful year, with the publication of CGAL version 6.1.

Here are a few of the features that were introduced during that year!

<br>
<ul style="list-style: none; padding: 0;">

  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/cdt_title_pyramid.png" alt="3D Constrained Triangulations"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>3D Constrained Triangulations</h3>
        <p>A major milestone for the CGAL library, this new package enables the construction
          of 3D Delaunay triangulations that can preserve a user-defined set of segments
          and 3D polygons.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-06-30-Constrained_triangulation_3.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Manual/packages.html#PkgConstrainedTriangulation3">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/996816-no-self-intersections.png" alt="Polygon Repair"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 2em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>Fixing Self-Intersections in Polygon Soups and Meshes</h3>
        <p>With CGAL 6.1, we introduced a new method for reliably resolving self-intersections,
        a very common defect in real-world meshes, using 3D snap rounding.
        The algorithm was thoroughly tested on large data sets such as <a href="https://ten-thousand-models.appspot.com/">Thingi10k dataset</a>,
        producing intersection-free outputs in all cases.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-06-13-autorefine-and-snap.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Manual/packages.html#PkgPolygonRepair">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/planar_remeshing.png" alt="Almost Planar Patch Remeshing"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>Remeshing of (Almost) Planar Patches</h3>
        <p>A new function was introduced to simplify planar patches, with a tolerance
        available to take into account inherent inaccuracies in real-world inputs.
        Individual functions are also provided for the different steps of the remeshing process (detection of the planar regions, detection of the corners, ...) to enable fine tuning
        of the algorithm and customization with user-specific criteria</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-05-22-Surface_remeshing.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/6.1/Polygon_mesh_processing/index.html#decimate">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/PolygonRepair_WindingNonZeroDifferent.svg" alt="Polygon Repair"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>Polygon Repair</h3>
        <p>This new package offers automatic repair of invalid polygons, polygons with holes,
        and multipolygons with holes through a fast and generic implementation,
        with immediate compatibility with other polygon-based CGAL packages.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-05-22-Polygon_repair.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Manual/packages.html#PkgPolygonRepair">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/isosurfacing_teaser.png" alt="Classical Contouring Methods"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>3D Isosurfacing</h3>
        <p>Classical methods for generating polygonal surface meshes have been added to CGAL:
        Marching Cubes, Dual Contouring. The new package follows the usual principles of CGAL,
        offering robustness, genericity with respect to the input data type, and various otions
        to tune the algorithms. A newer contouring method is also part of this initial version,
        offering a modified Marching Cubes approach that comes with topological guarantees.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-10-10-Isosurfacing.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Manual/packages.html#PkgIsosurfacing3">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/ACVD_ex-mini.png" alt="ACVD Remeshing"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>Remeshing with Approximated Discrete Centroidal Voronoi Diagrams</h3>
        <p>The result of a successful Google Summer of Code project in CGAL, this new remeshing
        function can be used to improve mesh quality and sharpen features. The algorithm
        combines the robustness and theoretical strength of Delaunay criteria with the efficiency
        of entirely discrete geometry processing, with a low complexity (in terms of calculations
        and memory requirements), allowing the processing of large meshes up to several million triangles.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-05-22-Surface_remeshing.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Polygon_mesh_processing/index.html#title8">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/clipping_carrot.jpg" alt="Faster Surface Clipping"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>Speeding-up Surface Mesh Clipping and Splitting</h3>
        <p>Clipping and splitting of polygonal surface meshes received a 10x acceleration,
        with a brand new corefinement function under the hood based on efficient
        and exact orientation tests.</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-06-06-new_clip.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>
          <i class="bi bi-book"></i>
          <a href="https://cgal.geometryfactory.com/CGAL/doc/master/Polygon_mesh_processing/group__PMP__corefinement__grp.html#ga5dc9e7acf8433235adc7b91ac93a7d42">Documentation</a>
        </p>
    </div>
  </li>

  <br>
  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/triangulation-on-hyperbolic-surface-mini.svg" alt="Triangulations on Hyperbolic Surfaces"
         style="width:45%; height:auto; margin-right: 1em; flex-shrink:0; max-width:100%;">
    <div>
      <h3>2D Triangulations on Hyperbolic Surfaces</h3>
        <p>The new package introduces a data structure and algorithms for
triangulations of closed orientable hyperbolic surfaces.  It is thus a
generalisation of the specific case of the Bolza surface, which is the most
symmetric hyperbolic surface of genus 2</p>
        <p>
          <i class="bi bi-pen"></i>
          <a href="{% link _posts/2025-06-24-triangulations-on-hyperbolic-surfaces.md %}">Full post</a>
          <br>

  </li>

  <li style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 2em;">
    <img src="../../../../images/frechet_distance.png" alt="Computation of Frechet Distances"

    <div>
      <h3>dD Frechet Distance</h3>
        <p>This package provides functions for computing an approximation of the Fr√©chet distance
        of two polylines, as well as a near neighbor data structure for polylines,
        both in any dimension under the Euclidean metric. The algorithm is both fast
        and provably correct.</p>
