CGAL Turns 30: Two Special Events for the Open-Source Community

2026 is a big year for CGAL! We celebrate the 30th anniversary of the project alongside our 50th CGAL Developer Meeting. To mark these milestones, we invite you to two public, half-day events dedicated to the world of open-source:

  • Technical Showcases: in-depth technical presentations of three major geometric libraries CGAL, VTK, SOFA
  • Panel Discussion: Open-Source, More Than Just a License: strategies and challenges of open-source projects

Schedule and Venue

Where: Inria Centre at Université Côte d'Azur, Sophia Antipolis (France)

When: April 23th, 2026

Program

09:00 - 09:15 | Introduction: a word from [...]

Technical Presentations

In the morning session we will take an in-depth look at the features of some geometry-related Open-Source projects.

09:15 - 10:00 | CGAL

Sebastien Loriot, GeometryFactory

CGAL is an open-source software library that provides geometric algorithms in the form of a C++ library. The algorithms range from spatial search stuctures, over point set processing, mesh generation, to polygon mesh processing. CGAL is used in various areas with a need for geometric computation, such as geographic information systems, computer aided design, molecular biology, medical imaging, computer graphics, and robotics.

10:00 - 10:45 | SOFA

Hugo Talbot, INRIA

SOFA is an open-source software designed for interactive mechanical simulation, with a focus on biomechanics and robotics. The software project is recognized for its ability to model the dynamics and interactions of rigid and deformable bodies. This makes it an ideal tool for creating digital twins.

10:45 - 11:15 | Break


11:15 - 12:00 | VTK, Paraview, Vespa, ...

Guillaume Gisbert, Kitware

The Visualization Toolkit (VTK) is open-source software for manipulating and displaying scientific data. It provides state-of-the-art tools for 3D rendering, a suite of widgets for 3D interaction, 2D plotting capability, and advanced data exploration features. ParaView is an open-source, cross-platform data analysis and visualization application based on VTK. Vespa enables the integration of CGAL algorithms into the VTK ecosystem. The filters can be used either as part of a VTK module or as plugins in ParaView.

12:00 - 14:00 | Lunch Break


Panel Discussion: Open-Source, More Than Just a License

While the morning session was about technology, the afternoon session adresses organizational aspects of Open Source.

We will see quite different models of Open Source projects linked to academia, from the single developer to large teams of contributors, and from volunteer work to funded projects.
